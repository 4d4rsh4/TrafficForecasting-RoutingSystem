System Architecture & Methodology

Urban traffic congestion has become a critical challenge, resulting in massive economic losses, environmental degradation, and increased commute times. Traditional traffic management systems are inherently reactive—addressing bottlenecks only after they have already formed.

This Major Project proposes a proactive, AI-driven solution. By leveraging historical sensor data (PeMS) and advanced Deep Learning architectures, our system forecasts congestion patterns before they happen. These predictions are actively fed into a dynamic routing engine, empowering users and city planners to mitigate gridlock preemptively.

Graph Convolutional Networks

Traditional algorithms fail to understand physical road layouts. We model the city as a mathematical graph, utilizing GCNs to capture the complex spatial dependencies and physical connectivity between intersections.

Gated Recurrent Units (GRU)

To understand the flow of time (rush hours, weekdays vs. weekends), we employ GRUs. This advanced RNN architecture solves the vanishing gradient problem, allowing the model to learn deep temporal sequences from historical traffic flows.

Predictive A* Routing

Unlike standard GPS that uses current distance, our system modifies the edge weights of the road graph using the model's future time predictions. The A* algorithm then computes the mathematically optimal alternate route to avoid future congestion.
