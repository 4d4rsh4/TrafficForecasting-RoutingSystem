About

Traffic congestion is a condition that occurs when the number of vehicles exceeds the capacity of a road or an intersection. It is characterized by vehicular pile ups, slower speeds and longer trip times. Traffic congestion has increased significantly since the 1950s. This has resulted in many urban roads becoming disused. Mathematically, traffic flow is often modeled similarly to fluid dynamics as a flow through fixed points on the route. Traffic congestion is a growing problem in many cities around the world. Rapid urbanization, increasing vehicle numbers, narrow road networks and inefficient traffic management lead to frequent traffic jams, longer commute times, higher fuel consumption, and increased environmental pollution. These issues reduce productivity and the quality of life for citizens. Traditional traffic management methods are mainly reactive and rely on manual monitoring or historical averages, which fail to analyze congestion patterns accurately. These systems also rely on fixed signal timings that do not adapt to changing traffic conditions, resulting in inefficient traffic flow and congestion. This makes it difficult to implement timely interventions or for commuters to plan optimally. This project aims to develop a predictive model using machine learning algorithms to forecast congestion levels in advance and use it to control traffic signals to reduce congestion or even avoid it altogether.
